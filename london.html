History of London

The city of London was founded by the Romans and their rule extended from 43 AD to the fifth century AD, when the Empire fell. During the third century, Londinium, the name given to the town by the Romans, had a population of 50,000, mainly due to the huge port. London has had many problems, such as the great plague of england and the great fire of london, which both destroyed the city and its population, but london still made it out and is the most popular city in the UK!



What can I do in London?





1. Go see London's incredible mueseums

London has got many different museums, such as-
1. Natural history museum- A museum that shows many different specimens from different spiecies
2. London Transport museum- A museum tht shows the history of brithish transport vehicles!
3. Science Museum- A museum that has many interactive workshops as wel as a huge science galleries- You could spend a whole day at this museum!
4. British museum- The British Museum is a public museum dedicated to human history, art and culture of London.
5. The National Gallery- An exebiton showing 2300 paintings from mid 13 century all the way to the 1900's!





2. Visit London's London eye and Big Ben

London eye and Big ben are London's most iconic place to go!
Have a look at Big Ben, and admire the structure which was constructed 23 years ago! Once your done, walk to the London eye and take in the scenic views of this huge wheel- or, if you want, you can ride this wheel and see London from a breathtaking birds-eye view!





3. Eat until you can't at Borough Market!

Located right in the heart of London, Borough Market is one of the best known produce and street food markets around. Open six days a week, the food market's atmospheric halls and passageways are a pleasure to explore. Pick from a variety of 100 different stalls, from fish, to bread to desserts, you can explore the many different and unique tastes the markets has!





4. Iconic figures at Madame Tussauds!

Located in the center of London, MAdame Tussauds has replicas of many dfferent famous figures! From Barack Obama to David Attenborough to even Indian political legend Mahatma Gandhi! Madame Tussauds has lifesize figures of anyone you know!





5. Sea Life Aquarium, Shrek Adventures and The London Dngeons!

The Sea Life Aquarium - Located right next to Lodnon Waterloo station, The sea life aquarium has a variety of different,unique underwater animals! Walk around for a good 30-45 enjoyng these majestic underwater creatures! Shrek Adventures - RIght next to The sea life aquarium, is childrens favourite character Shrek! Shrek's adventures is a brilliantly bonkers, interactive and immersive walkthrough experience, where the whole family can journey through the world of shrek as the stars of the show.
