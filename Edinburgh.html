History of Edinburgh

Edinburgh has been the capital of Scotland since 1437, when it replaced Scone. The Scottish Parliament resides in Edinburgh. However, in the past, Edinburgh Castle was often under English control. Before the 10th Century, Edinburgh was under the control of the Anglo-Saxons and Danelaw.

What can I do in Edinburgh?





1. Edinburgh Castle

Edinburgh Castle is a ancient castle, last stayed in overnight by King Charles the 1st in 1633.It has had a long history, serving as a royal residence, millitary garrison,A prison and a fortress! Nowadyas, It is used as a tourist attraction, which people visited a whopping 1.3 million times last year!





2. National Museum of Scotland

The National museum of Scotland consists of many different Scottish antiques, culture and history of the cool country!





3. Arthur's seat

Arthur's seat is the remains of an extinct volcano overlooking the city of Edinburgh. Take in the Amazng scenic views as you walk around this massive volcano





4. Camera Obscura and World of Illusions

At Camera Obscura & World of Illusions, experience five floors of interactive hands-on optical illusions, tricks and fun things to do including vortex tunnel, mirror maze and shrinking room.





5. The Chocolatarium

Fan of chocolate! You're going to love this place! This is 90 minutes of chocolate heaven. Find out how chocolate is made, make your own dream chocolate bar to take home and enjoy a session in the Tasting Room with over 40 kinds of chocolate!
