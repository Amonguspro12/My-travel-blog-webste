Bath



History of Bath

Founded by the Romans as a thermal spa, Bath became an important centre of the wool industry in the Middle Ages. In the 18th century, under George III, it developed into an elegant town with neoclassical Palladian buildings, which blend harmoniously with the Roman baths.

What can I do in Bath?





1. The Historc Roman Baths

The Roman Baths is one of the finest historic sites in Northern Europe. The Roman baths, which were once used for the luxury spa days, are now a facinatng toursist attraction, with 1 million visitors a year!





2. Relax at Thermae Bath Spa

You can't take a dip in the Roman Baths, but you can take a dip in the luxorious,warm and calming waters in the Thermae Bath Spa. This is a modern day, exotic spa with soothing treatments, relaxation spaces and a spectacular rooftop pool with a 360 degree view of the city!





3. The Pump room Restaurant

Once your done with the Roman Baths, Eat a meal in the fancy, luxorous pump room, where you can listen to classical music, enjoy a coffee, lunch or afternoon tea, all while looking over the magnificent Baths





4. Get a birds-eye view of bath in a balloon!

Want a fun, thrilling activity to do with family or friends? Great! Get ready to soar over the streets of bath as you sit and relax in a comfy hot-air balloon!





5. Visit Pulteney Bridge

One of the most photographed examples of Georgian architecture in the city and one of only four bridges in the world to have shops across its full span on both sides, Pulteney Bridge was made in 1769! The bridge is named after Frances Pulteney, wife of William Johnstone Pulteney, the UK's thought-to-be wealthiest man .
