History of Manchester

Manchester was created when a wooden fort was constructed by the roman army around 79 AD. The Romans called it Mamucium (breast-shaped hill) probably because the plateau resembled a breast.This fort wasa then rebuilt 150 years later with stone, and slowly a civilasatiion began to grow. The soldiers made a market for certain items like shoes and wine- however, around 400AD the romans left the UK and the stone fort fell to ruins.
Again, in 919 the king repaired the old Roman fort as a defense against the Danes. It gave its name to Castlefield. By the 12th century, things were changing rapidly. England got wealthier and slowly, manchester grew into a town. Eventually, Manchester was considered a city. And that's the story on Manchester



What can I do in manchester?





1. Visit Manchester's very own science and industry museum

Manchester has got a reputation of being the birthplace of industry. Check out the ideas that changed the world in the Revolution Manchester gallery. Also admire manchester's still active cotton legacy in the Textiles Gallery.


2. Etihad Stadium

Visit Manchester's football stadium, that can hold up to 55,000 people- makinbg it the 10th largest stadium in the UK!


3. Manchester art gallery

Fan of art? Visit Manchester art Museum, a display introducing the gallery including its origins, how the collection was formed and how it relates to Manchester and its people.


4. HOME

With five cinema screens, two theatres and an art gallery, HOME is an open and social space for the curious from all our communities, celebrating artists and work that is provocative, playful and contemporary, of Manchester and the world.


5. Castlefield

Castlefield, is the site in which the Roman era fort of Mamucium or Mancunium which gave its name to Manchester.
